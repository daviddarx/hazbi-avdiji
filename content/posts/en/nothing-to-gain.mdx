  - content: >
      From a purely utilitarian perspective, competencies in diversity will give
      you an advantage over others. The economic power of most marginalized
      groups is growing year on year.


      We now know that marketing teams with D\&I competencies [outperform others](#media-13-marche).


      We can also mention the risk that our discriminatory remarks push away our
      partners. An illustration is [Emmanuel Macron's speech](#media-13-Macron)
      in Ouagadougou in 2017, where his colonial biases sparked the beginning of
      a series of frictions with certain African leaders.


      In the end, diversity competencies are increasingly like digital
      competencies: at first, people thought they were only the domain of
      engineers on the second floor, then digitalization began to be so
      omnipresent that everyone is now expected to have basic skills. The same
      goes for diversity: initially mainly the responsibility of equality offices
      or HR departments, and increasingly integrated into the competency profiles
      of [managers](#media-13-leaders), [project managers](#media-13-projet),
      product developers, marketing managers, etc.


      From a more spiritual point of view, not mixing with people who are
      different can limit our understanding of ourselves, others, and the world
      around us. Our relationship with life can become very uniform or even
      monochrome. We will also tend to blend into the mold of our social group
      and develop our identity according to pre-established patterns.


      The [list of consequences](#media-13-survival_strategies) is long.
