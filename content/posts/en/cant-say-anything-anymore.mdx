      I start a training session in a room of 40 people. During the first 15
      minutes, I explain that I would like to put some ground rules in place,
      including maintaining a balance of speaking time to avoid some people
      monopolizing it at the expense of others.


      That is when a participant lets slip: "so we can avoid women talking too
      much." I cite [a study](#media-07-temps_parole_general) showing that men
      have more speaking time in meetings than women but also feel that women
      speak far more than them. And the most interesting part is that
      under-qualified men speak much more than highly-qualified women [in their
      group](#media-07-temps_parole_qualifications).


      We all have our own vision of the world that we build from our emotions and
      experiences. But often, these are misleading because of all the biases that
      creep into our heads.


      The person in my training may have succumbed to negativity bias, which
      pushes us to give more weight to things we judge as negative. This
      participant very likely experienced his female colleagues speaking up as
      noise or a nuisance.


      This negativity bias is the one I see most frequently in people hostile to
      D\&I. Another typical example: [American conservatives](#media-07-gingerbread)
      who fear that American culture will be wiped out by inclusive language.


      We can also think of [confirmation bias](#media-07-biais_confirmation),
      which leads us to discard information that [contradicts](#media-07-unpleasant_truths)
      what we think.


      Another example: the Dunning-Kruger effect, which leads us to believe in a
      reality even when we have no knowledge of the subject, as shown by [this
      study](#media-07-perception_racisme) from the Swiss Federal Statistical
      Office on the lower perception of racism among the population that has no
      contact with racialized people.


      And the best antidote to these biases is [scientific studies](#media-07-science),
      which offer us a more rational understanding of reality. That is why Donald
      Trump insisted so much on the notion of [fake
      news](https://www.theguardian.com/news/2018/aug/03/denialism-what-drives-people-to-reject-the-truth?fbclid=IwAR3HknN1Xi77EKBEMm3IGC7AacIXLeGTynZvcJdSQaNtohxxqfg1kqduJwA)
      to [dismiss any study](#media-07-personal_opinion) confirming certain
      discriminations.


      Beyond all these biases, there is sometimes also bad faith that pushes
      people to cry "we cannot say anything anymore" simply because they are not
      allowed to make [discriminatory remarks](#media-07-sensitive).
