      I will come back to the importance of this topic in the workplace at the end.

      But first, let us start by defining what toxic masculinity is. And I do say toxic, because too often we get the impression that all masculinities are being targeted.

      Toxic masculinity is a masculinity that has harmful consequences for oneself and others. It is a dominant, conservative, and restrictive masculinity.

      It is restrictive because it will punish anyone who transgresses its codes, no matter how trivial. Men will, for example, forbid themselves from drinking out of certain [cocktail glasses](#media-42-cocktails), from applying [lip balm](#media-42-baume) in a certain way, or will only buy [beauty products](#media-42-dude_wipes) that seem masculine enough. [One more little bonus](#media-42-restriction).

      Toxic masculinity is also a fairly fragile masculinity. This combination of restriction and fragility creates an explosive cocktail. Toxic masculinity, when threatened, leads to violence towards others, as illustrated by this video of a [fashion influencer](#media-42-influenceur), [cyberbullying](https://www.ldh-france.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/DL203-Actualite-2.-Cyberviolences-de-genre.pdf) of women and LGBTI people, or the [crimes](#media-42-crime) it gives rise to.

      If you are interested, decades of research on the subject have drawn up a fairly exhaustive [list of consequences](#media-42-grc2) of toxic masculinity.

      In the workplace, this leads to [toxic leadership](https://spssi.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/josi.12284), which has a [very high cost](https://hbr.org/2015/12/its-better-to-avoid-a-toxic-employee-than-hire-a-superstar) for the company as a whole. For colleagues, it is an environment dominated by competition, pressure, sexist jokes, and a lack of social skills.

      But this can change, particularly with different role models. Some influencers have specialized in promoting a masculinity that deconstructs the restrictions that toxic masculinity imposes, like [this](#media-42-fleurs) or [that](#media-42-hommelette). I also invite you to check out the services offered by the [mencare](https://www.maenner.ch/mencare/) association. All of this will help make the (professional) world less authoritarian and oppressive for everyone, including men.
