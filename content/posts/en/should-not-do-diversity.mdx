      Some people with ideas opposed to wokism have been feeling emboldened lately in Europe. They block with the first argument that comes to mind, most often the fact that companies are not a charity.

      What I like to remind people of in those moments is that diversity has its advantages, whether one is for or against it:

      * [9% higher EBIT margins](https://hbr.org/2018/01/how-and-where-diversity-drives-financial-performance) and [better financial returns](#media-15-returns)
      * [better decision-making](#media-15-decision) and [fewer fatal errors](#media-15-erreurs)
      * [better market understanding](#media-15-marche)
      * more [creativity and innovation](#media-15-creativite)
      * [better staff retention](#media-15-retention)

      What I personally find most interesting is that these teams are better equipped to navigate [VUCA environments](#media-15-VICA).

      We can also view the benefits of diversity and inclusion as the costs of homogeneity and exclusion. Not doing diversity and inclusion has a cost. It costs us 9% lower EBIT margins, it costs us in poorer-quality decisions, in fatal errors, etc.

      Often, this reversal of perspective hits home.

      Finally, I invite you to watch [this episode](https://latele.ch/emissions/diversite/diversite-s-2025-e-1) of the Diversité program on La Télé where I comment on the anti-woke movement in the United States and the [open letter](https://www.federationdesentreprises.ch/actualite/lettre-ouverte-61-patrons-romands-sengagent-contre-laffaiblissements-des-politiques-de-diversite) written by around sixty SME leaders in French-speaking Switzerland.
