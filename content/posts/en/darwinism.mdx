      One of the obstacles we must overcome to free ourselves from prejudices about people from the Global South is the [Darwinian bias](#media-10-darwin). We get the impression that humanity's evolution occurred in a linear way: from hunting and gathering, humanity moved to agriculture, then to empires, then to the industrial revolution, and now to modernity. And we believe that dominant groups are those that are the most virtuous, typically Western countries.

      What reinforces our belief in this bias is that we are no longer in the era of colonization. So when we see that among the [100 most valuable companies](#media-10-100) in the world, almost 90% are in the West, it must be because we are more competent and gifted.

      But when we look more closely, it is rather that we are the region of the world that has made the most use of power.

      On one hand, even after independence was granted to countries of the Global South, the use of force remained but disguised itself in another form. We speak today of neocolonialism when Western countries use their dominance (through financing coups or corrupting local political elites) to impose the privatization and liberalization of natural resources in Global South countries. This clears the way for large Western corporations to draw on [the resources of other continents](#media-10-neocolonialisme). The latest example is the [Tilenga project](#media-10-tilenga) in Uganda, which forces approximately 100,000 people to relocate without compensation or consultation.

      For those who want to go further, you can also look at the link between major multinational institutions (e.g., the International Monetary Fund, World Bank) and Western access to resources. The aid from these institutions was given in exchange for advantages for the West.

      In the end, according to some estimates, the value of resources appropriated by the West is 30 times greater than the aid it has given to the [Global South](#media-10-drain).

      From our position in the West, it is difficult to realize these dynamics that are hidden from us yet well known to the countries that are their target. But with changing geopolitical relations, these dynamics are increasingly being brought to light. We realize that this [international community](#media-10-communaute) we have spoken of until this decade represented only the minority of the [most powerful countries](#media-10-Ukraine). As a bonus, a more restricted community on [Palestine's admission to the UN](#media-10-Palestine).

      The West is no longer the default region.
