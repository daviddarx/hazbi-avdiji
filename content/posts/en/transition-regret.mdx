      It is true that this is a golden argument if you want to send the message
      that gender-affirming treatments should be banned or should not be covered
      by health insurance. If it is indeed a frequent problem, we might as well
      take it seriously and discuss how to address it as a society.


      Fortunately, we have estimates of transition regrets: a [tiny percent](#media-73-regret_trans_1).
      If we get the impression the percentage is higher, it is due to media
      manipulation of opinion. Many programs that covered the topic showed one
      person who accepted their transition and one who regretted it. This way of
      presenting information makes you think that regret concerns half of all
      trans people when the reality is 50 times smaller.


      But even so, one could say that just because the percentage is low does not
      mean we should not talk about it. In that case, what can help is to compare
      the phenomenon to others to see what is done about other types of regret.


      So, a little quiz.


      Do you know how many people who have had knee replacement surgery regret the
      operation? [About 10%.](#media-73-regret_genou) We should therefore have
      10 times more media coverage of the risk of regret for knee replacements
      than for gender transitions.


      But that is not all. The proportion of trans men regretting their
      mastectomies [is 1%](#media-73-regret_mastectomie_trans), whereas that of
      women who had a mastectomy after breast cancer is [5% to 14%](#media-73-regret_mastectomie_femmes)
      according to estimates.


      Let us continue with other life-altering decisions outside the medical
      field. In the United States, 12% of people [regret their tattoos](#media-73-regret_tatouage).
      Or again, it is estimated that between 7% and 18% of people regret
      [having children](#media-73-regret_enfants) in Germany and the United
      States. Does that mean we force parents to undergo psychological testing to
      see if they really want children?
