      What is great is that we can easily build entire training sessions on implicit biases and make them stimulating. I have done it countless times and it works very well.

      You will be surprised by your own brain through [various exercises](#media-23-trolley). You will realize that you do not think rationally and that if there are certain injustices, it is because our brains are wired to [play favorites automatically](#media-23-biais).

      Bonus: if everyone is like that, then there is no need to feel guilty about injustices in your company.

      The problem is when bias training remains the only initiative put in place. No HR recruitment or promotion process is changed to remove [barriers to hiring](#media-23-obstacles). No representation targets in management positions are set. No discussion is started on organizational or architectural accessibility.

      And we end up wondering whether it has really borne fruit.

      Although training sessions are the best entry point for companies wishing to address diversity and inclusion issues, they remain just that: an entry point. For the benefits of diversity to manifest, a strategic approach to D\&I is needed, otherwise we stay on the surface checking boxes.

      But sometimes reality is full of constraints, whether in terms of budget, lack of support from management, or resistance from some employees.

      Whether you are ready to embark on a strategic program or can only afford training sessions, I would be happy to guide you through the [services](https://www.avdiji.com/services) I offer.
