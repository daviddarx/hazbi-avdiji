      One of the things few of us had seen in the 2010s is how much we had not realized that excluding men from feminist movements was going to create quite a severe backlash. This backlash is today called [masculinism](#media-43-masculinisme).

      Masculinism conceives of itself as the equivalent of feminism but for men, namely a movement that comes to the aid of men, who are increasingly disadvantaged in our Western societies. According to many masculinists, feminism has even worked [against their interests](#media-43-control), hence the need for a movement of their own.

      I concede two things to masculinism. First, I genuinely believe that a large number of men are fragile today. Second, I also believe that keeping men at a distance from feminist circles carried certain risks.

      But at the same time, could we have done it differently? There was a need to feel "among ourselves" in order to share and heal from suffered pain. It was difficult to take care of ourselves and at the same time educate those who were hurting us.

      Where I do not agree with masculinism, however, is on the causes of the problems men face. Masculinists make a major attribution error. They attribute the source of their problems to feminism when the culprit is the patriarchy. The guilty parties are actually [not who they think](https://www.raiv.ulaval.ca/sites/raiv.ulaval.ca/files/publications/fichiers/pub_113.pdf).

      When men blame women for losing custody of children during a divorce, they do not realize that these decisions are made on the basis of the stereotype "women take care of children." And unfortunately, this stereotype is actually [a reality](#media-43-enfants). And many feminist movements actually wanted more equitable custody.

      The same goes for men's mental health, which is said to be very fragile today. Many masculinists point to women as the culprits, but research shows that the greatest source of pressure on men today is… [patriarchy](#media-43-patriarcat). The very patriarchy that feminists themselves denounce.

      You may even hear that women are stealing men's jobs, when [that is false](#media-43-evolution). When we look at the statistics, we see that leadership positions (and therefore the best-paid ones) are [not being seized](https://www.crif.ch/fr/nouvelles-et-evenements/nouvelles/la-proportion-de-femmes-dans-le-management-est-de-28-4/) by women. What this argument does show, however, is that masculinism is [against other](#media-43-parite) social groups.

      When I talk about this with my students, I like to draw a Venn diagram where I first ask them to give me examples of masculinists who want to lift men up while pushing others down. Lots of [names come up](#media-43-venn1).

      When I ask them to give me names of masculinists who want to lift men up without pushing others down, [nothing comes to mind](#media-43-venn2). The lack of inclusive masculinists is such that one student even replied "well, you, you belong to that category."

      That says it all...
