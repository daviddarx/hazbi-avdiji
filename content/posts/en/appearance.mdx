      About half the time, when I mention appearance as a dimension that can influence professional careers, my interlocutors react with surprise.

      Yet appearance has a non-negligible impact in many professional spheres:

      * Women considered attractive have [16% more chance](https://www.ft.com/content/bff62e63-6a77-433e-b51f-3c7ce188c684) of raising funds for their startups.
      * People perceived as attractive are [judged](#media-91-attractiveness) as more intelligent, emotionally stable, confident, and reliable.
      * If you are a tall person, you will have more chances of landing an interview, with 72% of recruiters preferring people of [greater height](https://www.hbrfrance.fr/carriere/lapparence-physique-cette-discrimination-impensee-en-entreprise-60314).

      That is a lot of advantages for something not to be taken into consideration when it comes to recruitment, promotions, or evaluations.

      I leave you with a few videos discussing this beauty privilege, such as this one where [crimes](#media-91-crime) are more easily forgiven for attractive people, or the experience of [weight discrimination](#media-91-grossophobie) at work.
