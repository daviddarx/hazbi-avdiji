      One advantage English has over some other languages is that it distinguishes between two things: [gender and sex](#media-28-gender_sex).

      Sex is physical, gender is social.

      According to biologists, sex is determined by three factors. The first and most visible of the three: the genitalia. This is also how we define a baby's sex: we look at what they have between their legs. A penis? It is a boy! A vulva? It is a girl. The second factor concerns [hormones](#media-28-hormones). We often think of estrogen as defining female sex and testosterone as defining male sex. And finally, we have what we learned in school: chromosomes: XX for female sex, XY for male sex.

      But the biological reality is even more complex than that. Things are not so binary. There is a [multitude of intersex variations](#media-28-intersexuation), biological sexes that cannot be categorized as easily as male or female. Some intersex people have male genitalia but develop breasts during adolescence. Some people also have three XXY chromosomes. In the end, there are dozens of different sexes. Even on this basis alone, our vision of sex as binary no longer reflects reality, which is far more complex than our brains are prepared to accept. Not to mention the different sexual systems that exist among [animals and insects](#media-28-sex_animals).

      Now let us move on to gender.

      If gender is not physical, it must be something else. The best way to understand what defines a woman or a man is to think negatively. At what point do we judge that a man is stepping outside his role as a man? In some cases, it will be because he uses facial beauty products, in others because he cries and shows his emotions, or because he is physically affectionate with his best friend. These transgressions have not always been the same throughout history and they change from place to place depending on culture. What we see is that gender is social. It defines the roles, obligations, and ways of living that we consider tolerable and desirable, and this begins [from childhood](#media-28-enfance).

      Trans and non-binary people do not feel comfortable in these roles or feel restricted by them. They will then either begin a gender transition to live their lives according to a gender different from the one they were born with, or free themselves entirely from the notion of gender and live their lives according to their own aspirations and desires. And at that point, there are as many genders as one is willing to accept.

      And for those who are not convinced, I leave you with this video of an [unexpected ally](#media-28-Oussama_Ammar).
