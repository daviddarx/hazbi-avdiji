      We need to distinguish between two things: racism and hostility.

      Yes, it is possible that you have been insulted with "dirty Swiss" or "fragile white person." You then experienced anti-white hostility, which should in no way be tolerated. But this is not sufficient to speak of racism in the systemic sense.

      Racism is not limited to insults or prejudices. It is a system that discriminates in the [areas of employment](#media-50-cv), [access to housing](#media-50-logement), interactions with [law enforcement](https://www.lematin.ch/story/droits-de-l-homme-rapport-alarmant-sur-les-violences-policieres-en-suisse-romande-103257931), or [access to healthcare](#media-50-soins). And the list could be much longer.

      A Swiss person will not be treated worse than others in these areas [because they are Swiss](#media-50-philia), unlike Arab, Roma, Black, Albanian, or Jewish people.

      Racism is a system that legitimizes, renders invisible, and normalizes these practices.

      Prejudices about racialized people's bodies will legitimize providing them with less adequate care.

      Invisibilization is when organizations do things that go unnoticed. For example, deciding not to give a job to a person with a [Cameroonian-sounding name](#media-50-cv2) or inflating [insurance premiums](https://www.albinfo.ch/fr/les-tarifs-albanais-de-la-discrimination-ou-traitement-justifie/) for people of Albanian origin. Nobody outside the organization will notice. Out of sight, out of mind.

      Normalization is when nobody is outraged by any of this, because of the media, [politicians](#media-50-udc), and the [lack of social mixing](#media-50-mixite) in places of life, which means we no longer have any empathy for these groups of people.

      You now see the [difference](#media-50-rokhaya) between an isolated case of hostility and a structural dynamic.

      The argument of anti-white racism serves the cause of far-right movements. Bringing racism down to the level of interpersonal prejudice allows us to eclipse the [levels of discrimination](#media-50-niveaux) at the organizational and societal levels. It is very convenient to obscure the dynamics of discrimination that have the greatest impact.
