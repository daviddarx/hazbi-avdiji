      This belief is a relic of the industrial revolution, when people with physical and intellectual disabilities were often excluded from social life, being considered unproductive. This was due to the nature of work at the time, which was very physically and mentally demanding.

      Today, [77.5% of jobs](https://www.bfs.admin.ch/bfs/fr/home/actualites/quoi-de-neuf.assetdetail.35867538.html) are in the tertiary sector in Switzerland and are more accessible for many people with disabilities.

      The first step will be to identify which people with which disabilities you are able to welcome into your company. For this, several tools are available.

      There is, for example, the "[requirements profile](https://www.anforderungsprofile.ch)" initiative, which catalogues all the capabilities required for different vocational qualifications. For example, the interactive media designer qualification requires certain [personal requirements](#media-82-exigences_personnelles) such as concentration and creativity, as well as [physical requirements](#media-82-exigences_physiques) such as fine motor skills or good color vision.

      We can also use [the International Labour Organization guide](https://www.ilo.org/sites/default/files/wcmsp5/groups/public/%40ed_emp/%40ifp_skills/documents/publication/wcms_111486.pdf) to analyze the components of a work environment and position. For example, does the position you need to fill require lifting and carrying light or heavy loads? Does your work environment exclude people with allergies due to sometimes excessive dust levels? You can then share these job profiles with potential candidates, for whom it will be easier to assess whether the environment and the position are suited to them.

      There are many other initiatives being carried out for the inclusion of people with disabilities. For example, [FPra](#media-82-fpra), which are practical training programs accessible without financial barriers funded by disability insurance, from which 1,200 apprentices graduate each year. Or the [disability insurance workplace assistance](https://www.proinfirmis.ch/fr/guide-juridique/travail/moyens-auxiliaires-et-assistance-au-poste-de-travail.html), which covers the costs of acquiring equipment necessary for employees with disabilities.
