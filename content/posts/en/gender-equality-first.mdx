      This statement implies that other forms of discrimination (e.g., racism, ableism, transphobia) must take a back seat. This misconception can stem primarily from two sources: (1) ignorance of intersectionality, or (2) a strategy by some white women to distance themselves from other causes.

      Let's take intersectionality. This concept reminds us that discrimination is not experienced [in isolation](#media-41-intersection): [it intersects and amplifies](#media-41-intersectionality2). For example, a mother without a disability can, rightly, complain about a lack of childcare spots. A mother with a disability will also struggle to find childcare spots, but will additionally face inaccessible locations, difficulties in funding childcare, or prejudices about her capacity to be a parent.

      If we do not consider all forms of discrimination at the same time, we only create solutions for white, heterosexual, non-disabled, middle-class women. All others will have only half-measure solutions (e.g., subsidized housing that is barely accessible for women in precarious situations with disabilities) or solutions that are not adapted at all (e.g., free gynecological consultations but without female gynecologists to care for Muslim women). Intersectionality instead allows us to think about problems [in a more adapted way](https://360.ch/suisse/65821-migrants-lgbtqi-les-exigences-du-sem-ne-collent-pas-a-la-realite/?fbclid=IwAR1idEZXCOrUvc0E1Kf-f8J9YJBa5t6494lNYJSphNzS4Cn5oB9hChWlQYE).

      Now let's look at the second issue. The idea that patriarchy is women's number one enemy is sometimes used by some women to avoid questioning their own privileges and the systems of oppression in which they participate. Indeed, white women are to anti-racist struggles what men are to feminism: [part of the problem](#media-41-probleme). Some white women prefer to focus on the system that oppresses them so as not to speak about the systems from which they benefit (e.g., racism, ableism).

      This phenomenon has a name: the [race to innocence](#media-41-innocence). People who experience only one type of discrimination seek to retain the role of oppressed (and therefore innocent) person by hiding the fact that they benefit from other systems of oppression. We cannot always be the one affected. We must also, at times, be an ally.

      But rest assured, this is often a necessary passing phase that can easily be overcome. Do not hesitate to contact me if needed.
