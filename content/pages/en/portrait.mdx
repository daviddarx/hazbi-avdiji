      ## A person affected by discrimination and privilege


      I have an ambivalent social position, which means I know what
      **discrimination** is, what it feels like to face more obstacles, challenges,
      or exclusion than others. But I also have my share of **privileges** that
      have allowed me to be here today, speaking to you as a consultant and
      lecturer at the University of Lausanne and the HEG Geneva.


      I have the privilege today of being able to understand the psychology of
      both sides. Knowing what creates fear on one side of the scale, what
      creates resignation on the other. This dual perspective allows me to
      practice **non-judgment** towards people who do not experience discrimination,
      while also having **legitimacy** with those who do.


      ## Sociological and economic knowledge


      My academic and professional background has always combined economics and
      sociology. This is what characterized my **doctoral thesis at HEC Lausanne**
      on cognitive diversity in work teams and made it so unique in the eyes of
      all the experts who reviewed it.


      Imagine what it can produce in the field of D\&I when only one or the other
      of these facets is considered. Focusing solely on the economic dimension
      would create hollow approaches like the "**managerial bullshit**" we hear
      everywhere. Focusing solely on the social dimension would create fine
      speeches that result in no concrete change within organizations.

      ## Generalist approach


      My ancestors who were peacemakers gave me an **egalitarian vision of the
      world**: some lives are not worth more than others. I remember that everyone
      was welcome at my grandparents' home, whether they were family or from
      another village, autistic or not, rich or poor.


      Treating everyone the same way has led me to develop this **generalist**
      (or even intersectional) **approach**. Gender equality is no more important
      in my eyes than the inclusion of people with disabilities or the integration
      of people with a migration background. Not prioritizing discriminations means
      my work does not create second-class citizens, no "it's always about LGBT,
      never about foreigners."


      ## Beyond categories, beyond the prefabricated


      I live in the countryside and work in the city. One foot in French-speaking
      Switzerland, the other in German-speaking Switzerland. On weekends I can
      enjoy calling out bingo numbers just as much as being on stage behind CDJs.
      The people who inspire me can be international celebrities as much as the
      village shopkeeper who decided to live her dream. I enjoy navigating
      **outside the codes** of masculinity and femininity as much as I can.


      In short, I have a natural drive that leads me to go **beyond pre-made
      categories**, to embrace the **complexity** of life, not to be afraid of
      opposites. You will surely have noticed in the misconceptions that I dissect
      prejudices using scientific studies, press articles, but also non-academic
      objects like TikTok videos or memes.


      It is by building bridges that we destroy walls.
